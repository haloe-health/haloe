A note from Halima

Thank you for booking with me. Whether this is your very first hijama session or you've had cupping before, I want you to feel completely at ease from the moment you arrive. This guide tells you exactly what will happen, what to bring, and how to look after yourself afterwards.

If you're nervous — that's completely normal, and you wouldn't be the first. Every session is just you and me, in a private room. You set the pace, and I work around you.

Location

Halima comes to you — every session is mobile. Just have a comfortable, private space ready. Travel is free in Manchester city centre; outside it, it’s charged at the cost of a taxi.

How long to allow

Add 20–30 minutes on top of your treatment time for the health form at the start and rest time at the end. A 1-hour session means roughly 1h 30m in total.

Payment

A £25 deposit is paid online when you book; the balance is settled on the day, by cash or bank transfer. Your confirmation shows the full amount.

Before we begin — your safety

Wet cupping cannot be performed if you:

  • Are pregnant
  • Take blood-thinning or anticoagulant medication
  • Are insulin-dependent (Type 1 / insulin-pump) diabetic
  • Are undergoing chemotherapy or radiotherapy

Please also let me know if you have anaemia, a bleeding disorder, have recently had surgery, or are managing any significant medical condition. If you're unsure, bring your medication list and we'll go through it together — no need to have it memorised. When in doubt, we wait for your GP's go-ahead.

If you practise ruqya: please leave at least 24 hours between your ruqya and your hijama session.

What happens in your session

  1. We talk first — health form & a chat about what you need
  2. Warm & relax — massage to settle the muscles
  3. Cups applied — suction lifts the skin gently
  4. Gentle draw — tiny pricks (wet cupping only)
  5. Soothe & rest — black seed oil, then quiet rest time

The suction feels like a firm tug. The wet cupping pricks are tiny and quick — most women describe them as a light scratch. Most people find the whole session deeply relaxing.

What is hijama?

Hijama, or wet cupping, is one of the world's oldest natural therapies — a practice rooted in prophetic (sunnah) tradition. In dry cupping the skin stays completely intact: gentle suction only, ideal for facial work, tension and cosmetic use. In wet cupping (hijama), a few tiny, shallow pricks allow a small amount of stagnant blood to be drawn, supporting circulation, detoxification and deeper therapeutic work.

"The best of the remedies you have is hijama."

Wear loose, comfortable clothing that can be moved aside easily — you won't need to fully undress. Only the area being treated will be uncovered, and you'll be draped throughout. Your modesty is fully respected at every point. Bring a list of any medications you take. Everything else — oils, aftercare — is provided.

To take home

After every wet cupping session, you'll leave with a bottle of black seed oil to apply to the treated area. It soothes the skin, supports healing, and is a sunnah remedy in its own right.

The marks (wet cupping only)

Before & after

Before your session

  • Eat 2–3 hours beforehand, not right before
  • Drink plenty of water the day before and morning of
  • Shower beforehand if you can
  • Avoid caffeine, alcohol and heavy exercise

After your session

  • Rest 10–15 min before heading home
  • Keep the area clean and dry for 24–48 hours
  • Apply the black seed oil blend as guided
  • Avoid strenuous activity, swimming & saunas for 24h

A little tenderness or light marking afterwards is completely normal and usually settles within a few days. If anything's on your mind, message me anytime — and for anything medical, your GP is always the right first port of call.
